- Asked the big question: ditch the Hiwonder stack for LeRobot? Researched it properly. Answer: NO — no driver
  for Hiwonder's servo bus, imitation learning needs teleop demos + a GPU we don't have, and a learned policy just
  absorbs calibration error into its training data. Keep the stack, replace only the calibration layer with our own code.
- Built the PLANAR pipeline in armpi_voice (the week's big win): planar_calib + planar_pick.
  * planar_calib: the arm generates its own ground truth — carries a block to a grid of known robot XY points,
    releases, retreats to a fixed view pose, detects the block's pixel, re-grasps, moves on. Fits pixel->XY,
    saves ~/planar_map.yaml. Starts with an interactive TUNE phase (step jaws down 5mm at a time, set jaw pulse)
    because the mat's height in the IK frame is not knowable remotely — guessed constants dropped blocks mid-air.
  * planar_pick: view pose -> HSV detect -> map -> IK hover/descend/close/lift -> looks again to verify the grasp.
- Debug lessons (each found the hard way, all fixed in code):
  * Affine fit is WRONG for this camera — perspective is huge (12cm of mat = 328px near vs 153px far). Switched
    to a homography with bounded outlier rejection (max 1/3 dropped). Fits real data at ~4-10mm.
  * Detection must be restricted to the mat area (ROI from the calibration points) — largest-red-blob grabbed a
    background object once (v=122 when the mat starts at v~180). Every pick now saves /tmp/pick_debug.jpg.
  * Gripper facts (verified live): pulse 100 = wide open, higher = narrower, close on block = 540, pitch = 80
    (mined from Hiwonder's own pick_and_place call). Jaws must snap WIDE before every descent or the block wedges.
  * The arm DROOPS with reach: graded z compensation (+5mm past x=0.19, +10mm past 0.22). Learned this the
    painful way — it dug into the mat and loosened a gripper screw (fix: power off, arm goes limp, tighten).
  * Grasp verify: "block moved" is NOT success (a miss can knock it away); check same-spot first, then the mat.
- RESULT: first genuinely verified picks! Multiple "Got the red block!" runs confirmed real. Reach envelope
  mapped honestly: the arm can only grasp x≈0.13-0.22 — the far half of the mat is physically out of reach
  (IK refuses 0.25), so the working zone is now a known, markable rectangle.
- Side fixes: deepseek-chat is retired 24/07 -> renamed to deepseek-v4-flash everywhere (verified against
